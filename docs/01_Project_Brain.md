# Meraki House — Project Brain

**Version:** 1.0.0

**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

# 1. Project Overview

## Project Name

**Meraki House**

---

## Project Type

Organic Haircare & Bodycare E-Commerce Website

---

## Project Purpose

The application simulates a real-world e-commerce platform where customers can browse, purchase, and manage orders for organic haircare and bodycare products.

The codebase should follow production-level architecture and best practices.

---

# 2. Product Categories

Meraki House specializes in sustainable, handmade personal care products.

The initial product categories include:

### Haircare

* Shampoo Bars
* Conditioner Bars
* Hair Oils
* Hair Serums
* Hair Masks

---

### Bodycare

* Face Bars
* Body Wash Bars
* Soap Bars
* Body Scrubs
* Body Butters
* Lip Balms

---

### Accessories

* Bamboo Soap Tray
* Wooden Comb
* Travel Tin
* Cotton Storage Bag

These categories may be expanded in future versions.

---

# 3. Vision

To create a clean, elegant, and user-friendly online shopping experience for organic self-care products while demonstrating modern full-stack development practices.

---

# 4. Project Objectives

The application should allow users to:

* Browse products
* Filter products by category
* Search products
* View detailed product information
* Add products to wishlist
* Purchase products
* Manage orders
* Track delivery status
* Contact support

The application should also allow administrators to:

* Create products
* Update products
* Delete products
* Manage inventory
* Manage orders
* View customer details
* View revenue
* Update delivery status

# 8. Design Philosophy

Meraki House follows a **Modern Minimal Bohemian** design language.

The experience should feel:

* Calm
* Natural
* Organic
* Premium
* Elegant
* Spacious
* Sustainable

---

# 9. Development Philosophy

Every feature should be:

* Reusable
* Responsive
* Accessible
* Maintainable
* Scalable
* Consistent

Business logic should remain in the backend, while the frontend focuses on presentation and user interaction.

---

# 10. Long-Term Approach

It should be structured as it were a production-ready application.

The architecture should support future enhancements without requiring major redesigns.
